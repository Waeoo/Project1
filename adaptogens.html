American ginseng

Botanical name:

Panax quiquefolius

Family:

Araliaceae

Common Names:

Sang, seng, xi yang shen (Chinese)

Taste/Energy:

Sweet, bitter, slightly warm, moist

Parts Used:

Root and (less frequently) leaf

Location/Cultivation:

American ginseng native to eastern USA and Canada

Safety Rating:

3 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen, antioxidant, bitter tonic, mild central nervous system stimulant, mild demulcent, hypoglycemic agent, immune amphoteric

Constituents:

The active constituents include triterpene saponins, known as ginsenosides or panaxosides.



Ashwagandha

Botanical name:

Withania somnifera

Family:

Solanaceae

Common Names:

Winter cherry, asgandh (Hindi)

Taste/Energy:

Bitter, warm, dry

Parts Used:

Root

Location/Cultivation:

Ashwagandha is native to the drier subtropic regions of India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka and parts of Africa.

Safety Rating:

3 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antispasmodic, antitumor, diuretic, immune amphoteric, nervine, thyroid stimulant

Constituents:

Steroidal lactones, including withanolides A to Y, sitoindosides, alkaloids, including somniferine, withanine and anaferine.



Asian ginseng

Botanical name:

Panax ginseng

Family:

Araliaceae

Common Names:

Ren shen (China), Chinese ginseng, Korean ginseng

Taste/Energy:

Sweet, bitter, warm, moist

Parts Used:

Root

Location/Cultivation:

Asian ginseng originally grew in the mountainous forests of the eastern Manchuria, northern China, and Korea. It is probably extinct in the wild. Cultivated on a large scale in China and North and South Korea.

Safety Rating:

3 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen (stimulating), antiasthmatic, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, cardiotonic (mild), immune amphoteric, nootropic

Constituents:

A range of triterpenoid saponins known as ginsenosides or panaxosides, as well as panaxanes



Cordyceps

Botanical name:

Ophiocordyceps sinensis, Cordyceps militaris

Family:

Clavicipitaceae

Common Names:

Dong chong xia cao (Chinese for winter insect, summer grass), Chinese caterpillar fungus

Taste/Energy:

Sweet, slightly acrid, warm, moist

Parts Used:

Mushroom (fruiting body), mycelial extract

Location/Cultivation:

Cordyceps is gathered from the wild in alpine grasslands and in the foothills of the Himalaya Mountains in Tibet and Bhutan.

Safety Rating:

2.5 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen, antiasthmatic, antileukemic, antioxidant, hepatoprotective, hypocholesterolemic, immune amphoteric, nephroprotective, nervine

Constituents:

The cordyceps mushroom contains immunostimulating polysaccharides (galactomannans, cordycepic acid), amino acids, fatty acids, polyamines and ecdysterones.



Eleuthero

Botanical name:

Eleutherococcus senticosus (synonym: Acanthopanax senticosus)

Family:

Araliaceae

Common Names:

Ci wu jia (Chinese), wu jia shen (Chinese), Siberian ginseng, ezoukogi (Japanese)

Taste/Energy:

Sweet, slightly bitter, slightly warm

Parts Used:

Root and stem bark

Location/Cultivation:

Eleuthero grows throughout Siberia, northern China, Korea, and northern Japan

Safety Rating:

3 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen (mild, nonstimulating), antioxidant, hypocholesterolemic, immune tonic

Constituents:

The active constituents are believed to be a group of compounds known as eleutherosides A to G



Rhaponticum

Botanical name:

Rhaponticum carthamoides (synonym: Leuzea carthamoides)

Family:

Asteraceae

Common Names:

Maral root, leuzea

Taste/Energy:

Bitter, slightly sweet and resinous, cool, slightly dry.

Parts Used:

Root

Location/Cultivation:

Grows in the alpine and subalpine fields of the southern and eastern Siberia (Buryatia, Tuva and Altai regions), Kazakhstan, Mongolia and Xinjiang province in China.

Safety Rating:

2.5 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen (anabolic), antidepressant, antioxidant, antitumor, cardiac tonic, hepatoprotective, immune stimulant, nervine.

Constituents:

Rhaponticum contains compunds that are identical to insect hormones known as ecdysterones. A group of ecdysterones, known as levseins, are the most biologically active compounds in the herb. Some rhaponticum products are standardized to include only a specific compound, 20-hydroxyecdysterone.



Rhodiola

Botanical name:

Rhodiola rosea

Family:

Crassulaceae

Common Names:

Rose root, golden root, hong jing tian (Chinese name for R. crenulata)

Taste/Energy:

Sweet, slightly bitter, spicy, cool, dry

Parts Used:

Root

Location/Cultivation:

Rhodiola is native to the northern circumpolar regions of Canada, Scandinavia, and Siberia. Also found at higher elevations in the Alps, Pyrenees, and Carpathian Mountains in Europe. Cultivated in Canada, Finland, Sweden, and Russia.

Safety Rating:

3 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen (stimulating), antiarrythmic (protects against irregular heartbeats), antidepressant, antioxidant, antiviral, cardioprotective, immune system stimulant, mild central nervous system stimulant, nervine, neruoprotective.

Constituents:

Rhodiola contains rosavins (rosavin, rosin, rosarin), salidrosides, flavonoids/phenolics (rodiolin, rodionin, p-tyrosol), and fragrant monoterpenes such as geraniol.



Schisandra

Botanical name:

Schisandra chinesis

Family:

Magnoliaceae

Common Names:

Wu wei zi (Chinese), gomishi (Japanese), omija (Korean), Chinese magnolia vine

Taste/Energy:

Sour, sweet, salty, bitter, pungent, warm, dry

Parts Used:

Fruit and seed

Location/Cultivation:

Schisandra chinensis is grown in Liaoning, Jilin, Heilongjiang, and Hebei provinces in China. Another species, S. sphenanthera, which is less effective, is grown in southern China and used as a substitute for S. chinensis.

Safety Rating:

3 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen (calming), antiasthmatic, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, astringent, expectorant, hepatoprotective, immune amphoteric, nervine.

Constituents:

Lignans such as schisandrin B, gomisans, and schisandrol A are considered to be active constituents of schisandra. The berries also contain essential oils and vitamin C.



Shilajit

Pharmaceutical name:

Asphaltum

Family:

none

Common Names:

Shilajitu, Shilajeet, mimijo, mumiyo (Russian), mumie, momia.

Taste/Energy:

Bitter, slightly pungent, salty, warm. It smells like stale cow's urine.

Parts Used:

Pitch

Location/Cultivation:

Shilajit is a blackish-brown substance that exudes from rocks in the high mountains of India, Tibet, Nepal, China, Russia, Afghanistan, Bhutan and Kashmir.

Safety Rating:

2 out of 3

Properties:

Adaptogen, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antiulcerogenic, hypoglycemic, immune amphoteric; inhibits allergic response.

Constituents:

Shilajit is composed of humus, various minerals, dibenzo-alpha- pyrones, and organic acids such as fulvic, humic, benzoic, and hippuric acids.
